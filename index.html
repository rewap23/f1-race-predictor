How This Predictor Works

A machine learning model trained on 7 seasons of Formula 1 data, predicting the probability each driver finishes on the podium.

The Model

A Random Forest classifier — an ensemble of 200 decision trees trained on race results from 2018 to 2025. Each tree learns a slightly different set of patterns; their votes are averaged into a final podium probability per driver.

What the Model Can't Predict

The model predicts podium probability under normal race conditions. Some outcomes are structurally unpredictable from pre-race data:

  • Safety car periods and red flags that shuffle race order
  • Mechanical failures and reliability issues mid-race
  • Crashes that eliminate front-running drivers
  • Post-race penalty decisions by stewards
  • Unexpected wet weather or rapidly changing conditions

Example: at the 2026 Monaco GP, Gasly qualified P9 but podiumed after penalties were handed to other drivers on review — a result no pre-race model could have foreseen.
